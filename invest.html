Ways to Grow Your Money

Here are different ways people save and invest. Let's learn about each one!

🏦

Savings Account

Put your money in a bank and it slowly grows a little bit over time. Super safe - the bank protects it for you!

Super Safe
📦

Index Funds

Instead of picking one company, you invest in a big group of companies at once. It's like buying a little piece of many stores!

Pretty Safe
🏠

Real Estate

Owning a house or building and renting it out to people. Like being a landlord! Your property can become worth more over time.

Medium Risk
📈

Company Stocks

Buying a tiny piece of a company like Apple or Nike. If the company does well, your piece becomes worth more!

Medium Risk
🪙

Crypto

Digital money on the internet like Bitcoin. It can go way up or way down really fast. Very exciting but very risky!

Very Risky
💡

Starting a Business

Use your money to start your own lemonade stand, app, or shop! Could make a lot or lose what you put in.

Very Risky

Money Rules Every Kid Should Know

Follow these simple rules and you'll be a money genius!

  • 🛑

    Only use money you don't need right now

    If you need that money for something important, don't invest it. Only use money that you're okay not touching for a while.

  • 🥚

    Don't put all your eggs in one basket

    Spread your money around! If you put everything in one place and it goes down, you lose everything. But if you spread it out, you're much safer.

  • 🐢

    Slow and steady wins the race

    The best investors are patient. Don't try to get rich quick - let your money grow slowly over many years. That's how the pros do it!

  • 📚

    Learn before you spend

    Always understand what you're putting your money into. If someone says "trust me, it's easy money" - be careful! Do your homework first.

  • 🌱

    Start small like a seed

    You don't need a lot of money to start. Even $5 or $10 can be invested! Start small, learn, and grow your money garden over time.

  • 🧘

    Stay calm - don't panic!

    Sometimes your money will go down a little. That's totally normal! Don't freak out and take it all out. Be patient and it usually comes back up.
